Middleware Modernization — Proof of Concept

What was built, how it was evaluated, and what the results show.

This proof of concept evaluates whether a modern, cloud-native integration platform can replace a production BizTalk estate. Two use cases were implemented against representative integration patterns drawn from a documented BizTalk environment. Results are reported against four criteria: functional correctness, throughput and latency, observability, and security posture. The goal is to give the technical team and stakeholders the information needed to evaluate platform options without relying on vendor claims.

The POC also exists in the context of a platform decision: the organization is evaluating two integration approaches. This site documents both stacks, compares them against objective criteria, and identifies the questions that must be answered before a direction is chosen.

POC Success Criteria

Functional Correctness

End-to-end execution against a representative dataset, with outputs reconciled against the BizTalk baseline.

Throughput & Latency

Target non-functional profiles sustained under representative load, measured and reported through Application Insights.

Observability

Telemetry, dashboards, and alerting wired through Azure Monitor, Log Analytics, and Application Insights, reviewed by operations representatives.

Security Posture

Baseline scan through Microsoft Defender for Cloud, secrets managed through Key Vault, workload identity through Entra ID Managed Identities.

What Was Built

The BizTalk estate spans four business areas with a combined 76 BizTalk applications, 167+ orchestrations, and 309 maps. All patterns were reviewed. Two use cases were selected that cover the highest-risk architectural concerns across the full estate.
